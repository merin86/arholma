Welcome to Arholma


Arholma is an island in the Stockholm archipelago. The island has a long history and has undergone changes over the years. Only about 30 years ago there were over 70 permanent residents on the island. Today, that figure is closer to 30 or 40 permanent residents. Nowadays, most of the houses are owned by summer guests and during the summer there are close to 500 people living on the island.


Here there is a grocery store that is open all year round, a church and various community functions such as garbage collection, mail delivery, etc. A passenger boat goes to the mainland, at fixed times, a couple of times a day (more frequently during the summer).


Summer time: Several hostels open and there are restaurants and cafes. Various different activities are also offered on the island, such as cultural events, kayak rentals, saunas and much more. Various activities are also arranged for children, such as football practice, dance and drum lessons and theatres. Arholma is known for its midsummer celebration, with its distinctive midsummer pole, when many guests visit the island. There is also a song about the island, Arholmavalsen, which is relatively well known throughout Sweden.
